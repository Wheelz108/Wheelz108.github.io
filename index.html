Personal profile

The Myers Briggs personality test is wildly inconsistant and imprecise. While it can nail someone's peronality on the head the first time they take the test, future results will vary wildly for the same person depending on where they are in their life at that moment. Personally I have taken it at least three times at different points in my life, once at the beginning of high school, once at the end, and once now for this profile. My results in order were: Logician (INTP), Mediator (INFP) and Advocate (INFJ).

The Big Five personality test is a much more precise and accurate gauge of a person's personality traits. The test uses five distinct criteria to catergorise people, those being Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness and Neuroticism. Often these criteria are shortened to the acronym OCEAN. According to my test results I'm less than 5% extraverted which I expected. But I didn't expect the percentages of the other traits, especially how neurotic I apparently am. But to be honest I don't care about these results too much.

The DISC Assessment is commonly used by employers to gauge potential employees on which roles they would fit in a team. This test uses four criteria to gauge the examinee's personality; Dominance, Influence, Steadiness and Conscientiousness. Here in this test my highest scoring traits are my dominance and compliance and influence is my lowest at only 7%.

To be completely honest, I don't give a damn about these results. They quantify my personality sure, but I already know who I am. It's not like taking those test magically generates a personality or anything. They just give you a reason to justify the behaviour of bad people in my opinion. While you might be forced to take these test by a HR department or whatever, but I feel that you'll get a much better gauge on a person's mannerisms and personality by working with them or being around them rather than going off of the results of some dumb test.
